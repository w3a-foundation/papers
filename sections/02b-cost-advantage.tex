\subsection{Component 1 --- T+1 capital lockup (the largest single line)}

SEC Rule 15c6-1(a), as amended effective 28 May 2024, requires
T+1 settlement for the US securities market. DTCC reports approximately
\textbf{\$2.1 trillion in daily settlement volume}. The cost of holding
that volume captive for one calendar day, at the May 2026 Fed Funds
effective rate of 5\%, is:

\[
C_{\text{T+1}} = V \times \Delta t \times \frac{r}{365}
= \$2.1 \times 10^{12} \times 1 \times \frac{0.05}{365}
= \$287.67~\text{million/day} = \boxed{\$105.0~\text{billion/year}}
\]

On a Web3 Alliance L1 with Quasar consensus finality $\Delta t \approx
500~\text{ms} \approx 7 \times 10^{-4}~\text{days}$, the same arithmetic
yields:

\[
C_{\text{T+0}} = \$2.1 \times 10^{12} \times 7 \times 10^{-4} \times \frac{0.05}{365}
\approx \$201{,}370~\text{per day} \approx \$73.5~\text{million/year}
\]

\textbf{Annual saving: \$104.9 billion.} The settlement-speed-up factor is
$172{,}800\times$. Pro-rata to a single \$11.5T-AUM manager
(approximately 31\% share of the \$37T US ETF + mutual-fund market):
\textbf{\$32.6 billion/year}, of which \textbf{\$1.44 billion/year}
is direct working-capital release at a 25\% annual turnover rate.
